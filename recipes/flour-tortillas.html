Flour Tortillas

Ingredients

  • 240 g (2 cups) all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 30 g (2 tbsp) vegetable oil, coconut oil, or vegan shortening
  • 180 ml (3/4 cup) warm water

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Dough
  2. In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt.
  3. Add the vegetable oil (or other fat of choice) and mix with your hands or a fork until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

  4. Gradually add warm water, mixing until a soft, smooth dough forms. If the dough is too sticky, add a little more flour; if it’s too dry, add a small amount of water.

  5. Rest the Dough

  6. Cover the dough with a damp towel and let it rest for 20–30 minutes. This makes the dough easier to roll out and ensures softer tortillas.

  7. Shape the Tortillas

  8. Divide the dough into 8–12 equal pieces, depending on the desired size of your tortillas. Roll each piece into a ball.

  9. On a floured surface, roll each ball into a thin, round circle with a rolling pin. Aim for about 1/8-inch thickness or less.

  10. Cook the Tortillas

  11. Heat a dry skillet or griddle over medium-high heat. Place a tortilla on the hot skillet and cook for about 30–45 seconds on the first side until bubbles form.
  12. Flip the tortilla and cook for another 30–45 seconds, then flip again briefly to ensure even cooking. The tortilla should have light golden spots.

  13. Remove from the skillet and keep warm in a clean kitchen towel while cooking the remaining tortillas.

  14. Serve

  15. Serve warm with your favorite fillings, as a wrap, or with dips. These tortillas are pliable, soft, and perfect for tacos, burritos, or as a bread substitute.
