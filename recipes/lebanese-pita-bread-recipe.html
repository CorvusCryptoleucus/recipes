Lebanese Pita Bread Recipe

Ingredients

  • 500 g (4 cups) all-purpose flour (or 50/50 mix of all-purpose and whole wheat flour)
  • 7 g (2 tsp) salt
  • 7 g (2 1/4 tsp) active dry yeast
  • 5 g (1 tsp) sugar
  • 300 ml (1 1/4 cups) warm water (40°C/105°F)
  • 15 ml (1 tbsp) olive oil

Instructions

  1. Activate the Yeast

  2. In a small bowl, mix the yeast and sugar with warm water. Let it sit for 5–10 minutes until frothy.

  3. Mix and Knead

  4. In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt.
  5. Add the activated yeast mixture and olive oil (if using).

  6. Mix until a dough forms, then knead on a floured surface for 8–10 minutes, or until smooth and elastic.

  7. First Rise

  8. Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with a damp towel or plastic wrap, and let it rise in a warm place for 1–1.5 hours, or until doubled in size.

  9. Divide and Shape

  10. Punch down the dough and divide it into 8 equal portions.

  11. Roll each portion into a ball, then flatten into a disc about 6–7 inches in diameter and 1/4 inch thick.

  12. Preheat the Oven

  13. Preheat to 250°C (480°F) or the highest setting. Place a baking stone or an inverted baking sheet in the oven to heat.

  14. Bake the Pita

  15. Place the discs onto the preheated stone or baking sheet. Bake for 3–5 minutes, or until they puff up and lightly brown.

  16. Cool

  17. Remove and cool under a clean towel to keep soft.
