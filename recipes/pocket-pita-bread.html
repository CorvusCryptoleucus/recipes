Pocket Pita Bread

Ingredients

  • 240 g (2 cups) all-purpose flour (or a 50/50 mix of all-purpose and whole wheat flour)
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 7 g (2 1/4 tsp) active dry yeast
  • 5 g (1 tsp) sugar
  • 180 ml (3/4 cup) warm water (40°C/105°F)
  • 15 ml (1 tbsp) olive oil

Instructions

  1. Activate the Yeast

  2. In a small bowl, mix the yeast, sugar, and 60 ml (1/4 cup) of the warm water. Let it sit for 5–10 minutes until it becomes frothy.

  3. Mix the Dough

  4. In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt.
  5. Add the activated yeast mixture, the remaining warm water, and olive oil. Mix until a dough forms.

  6. Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 8–10 minutes, or until it is smooth and elastic.

  7. First Rise

  8. Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with a damp towel or plastic wrap, and let it rise in a warm place for 1–1.5 hours, or until it doubles in size.

  9. Divide and Shape

  10. Punch down the dough and divide it into 6–8 equal pieces. Roll each piece into a ball.

  11. Flatten each ball with a rolling pin into a disc about 1/4 inch (6 mm) thick and 6 inches (15 cm) in diameter. Cover the discs with a towel and let them rest for 15–20 minutes.

  12. Preheat the Oven

  13. Preheat your oven to 250°C (480°F) or the highest setting. Place a baking stone or inverted baking sheet in the oven to preheat as well.

  14. Bake the Pita

  15. Carefully place 2–3 dough discs onto the preheated stone or baking sheet. Bake for 3–5 minutes, or until they puff up completely. Do not overbake; they should remain soft and light golden.

  16. Remove from the oven and immediately wrap in a clean kitchen towel to keep them soft. Repeat with the remaining discs.

  17. Serve

  18. Allow the pita to cool slightly before cutting. They will have formed pockets, perfect for stuffing with your favorite fillings like falafel, hummus, or vegetables.

  • Calories: 140
  • Carbs: 28 g
  • Protein: 4 g
  • Fat: 2 g
  • Fiber: 1 g
